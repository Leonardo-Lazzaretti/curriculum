COMAU Industrial Robotics ✅ Certified intensive course

Certified course in the use and programming of COMAU industrial robots: axis movement, trajectory management, program logic, and safety principles in a robotic cell.

Project Description

COMAU Industrial Robotics Course – intensive course conducted on real robots in an educational cell, focused on programming, safety, and trajectory management. The course included an introductory theoretical part and numerous practical exercises directly on the COMAU robotic arm.

  • Use of the COMAU programming teach pendant to command the robot's movements.
  • Setting points, trajectories, and speeds on realistic work scenarios.

My role: course participant with a focus on the advanced technical part: I worked on movement programming, cell I/O management, and analysis of safety conditions for using the robot in an industrial environment.

What I did

- Robot programming: creation and modification of programs for point-to-point movements and continuous trajectories, management of speeds, accelerations, and interpolations.
- Cell setup: definition of coordinate systems, positioning of references, and verification of workspaces and safety zones.
- I/O management: use of digital signals to control grippers/actuators and synchronize the robot with external elements of the educational cell.
- Debugging and optimization: correction of possible collisions, path optimization, and reduction of cycle times in the proposed exercises.

Results and skills

Result: achievement of the COMAU certification in basic use and programming of the industrial robot, with a final practical test in the cell.

Skills developed:
• COMAU industrial robot programming (movements, I/O, basic logic)
• Awareness of safety regulations in a robotic cell
• Reading layouts and interpreting the physical constraints of a plant
• Technical problem-solving for path errors, software limits, and minor faults
• Ability to transfer robotics concepts to students during STEM lessons
