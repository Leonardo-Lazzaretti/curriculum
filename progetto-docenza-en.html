STEM Teaching and Training 2 years of experience

Technical teaching activities at ITS Luigi Einaudi: teaching Systems and Industrial Automation for the final three years of high school, afternoon PNRR-funded courses, Physics remedial classes, and active participation in graduation exams and incoming orientation.

Project Description

For two years, I have been performing STEM teaching activities in Systems and Industrial Automation for the third, fourth, and fifth-year mechanical classes at ITS Luigi Einaudi, independently managing lessons, assessments, and coordination with the class council.

In parallel, I have been involved in high-responsibility educational activities: internal examiner for the 2025 State Exam, point of contact for incoming orientation of future students, and instructor for the Solidworks 3D PNRR courses for all three senior years.

What I did

- Systems and Automation Teaching: annual curriculum planning, theoretical-practical lessons on sensors, actuators, PLC, and basic robotics, preparation of written and practical assessments for 3rd, 4th, and 5th-year classes.
- 2025 Graduation Exam Internal Teacher: grading of the second written major-specific exam, participation in oral interviews with technical-disciplinary questions, and contribution to collective decisions on final grades.
- Incoming Orientation and Open Days: welcoming middle school students and families, presenting the mechatronics educational offer, guided laboratory tours, and organizing "hands-on" laboratory afternoons to let visitors try out equipment and test benches.
- PNRR Solidworks 3D Courses: designing and leading 2 afternoon courses for the three senior classes, with basic modules for beginners (sketches, extrusions, simple assemblies) and advanced modules oriented toward the professional world (technical drawings, tolerances, small complete projects).
- Physics Remedial for the first two years: dedicated path for students with major deficiencies, reviewing fundamentals of kinematics, dynamics, and statics, and targeted preparation for the remedial exam before the start of the new school year.

Results and Skills

Main Results:
• Two consecutive years of stable teaching across three mechanical engineering classes.
• Appointment as internal teacher for the 2025 State Exam in Mechanics, with full involvement in written and oral tests.
• High participation in Open Days and orientation activities, with positive feedback from students and families.
• Completion of two cycles of Solidworks PNRR courses for the entire three-year senior period.

Skills developed:
• Technical pedagogy and classroom management in laboratory settings.
• Ability to explain complex concepts (automation, mechanics, Physics) at different levels, adapting language and examples.
• Design of structured training paths (annual programming, PNRR courses, remedial paths).
• Objective and collective assessment in exams, with attention to shared rubrics and criteria.
• Public speaking and communication with students, parents, and colleagues during Open Days, class councils, and exam sessions.
